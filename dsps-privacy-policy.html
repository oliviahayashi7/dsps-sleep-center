Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

This website, DSPS Sleep Center, is a free patient education resource about Delayed Sleep Phase Syndrome (DSPS). We are committed to being transparent about how this site works and what data, if any, is collected when you visit.

Who we are

DSPS Sleep Center is a free, non-commercial patient education website. We do not sell products, collect payment information, or require you to create an account.

What information is collected

We do not collect any personal information directly — you do not need to provide your name, email, or any other details to use this site.

However, this site uses Google Analytics to understand how visitors use the site. Google Analytics automatically collects:

  • Pages you visit and how long you spend on them
  • The general region or country you are visiting from
  • What type of device and browser you are using
  • How you arrived at the site (e.g. a search engine or direct link)

This information is anonymous — we cannot identify you personally from this data. It is used only to understand how the site is being used so we can improve it.

Cookies

Google Analytics uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to track visits. These cookies do not contain personal information and are used only for analytics purposes.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking at any time by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

How Google uses this data

Google may use the data collected through Google Analytics for its own purposes in accordance with Google's privacy policy. To learn more about how Google collects and processes data, visit: How Google uses information from sites that use our services.

Third-party links

This site contains links to external websites such as the American Academy of Sleep Medicine, Reddit, ClinicalTrials.gov, and others. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their own privacy policies.

Children's privacy

This site is intended for general audiences including teenagers and their families. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

Medical disclaimer

The content on this site is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment of any sleep disorder.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date at the top.

Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please reach out through the contact information listed on the main page.
